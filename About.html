How do we shape technology,
and how does technology shape us?

I've been asking myself this question for a while now.

It's a question I've subconsciously pursued throughout my career and life. My career path has moved across disciplines: from studying psychology to research, copywriting to digital marketing, from change management to community design. While the roles and titles have varied, a few things seem to remain consistent so far:

- I enjoy wrestling with systems and structures

- I try to optimise language into concise forms

- I've found myself stomping around in tech startups

Like right now for instance - I'm designing conversations at Versa.ai.

Outside of work, I find myself trying to make sense of this rapidly shifting world through symbols and myths. I value thoughtful and accessible design, precise and warm communication, analytical and curious attitudes. I use psychology, systems thinking, and long-term ethics to view questions like: How do we design and relate to digital systems? What kinds of patterns in machines and society are subtly shaping how we live and connect?

To me, it seems like the more we delve into machines, the more we reveal about the human condition. And about how little we actually know ourselves.

Welcome to this space of enquiry. Let's explore together.

Warmly,
Elisa

How do we shape technology,
and how does technology shape us?

I've been asking myself this question for a while now.

I feel like it's something I've subconsciously pursued throughout my life. My career's followed, moving across chapters like majoring in Psychology, digital marketing, community design. The roles and titles differ, but a few things remain consistent so far. I:

- enjoy wrestling with systems and structures

- like optimising sentences and language

- find myself working in tech startups

Right now, for instance, I'm designing conversations at Versa.ai.

Outside of work, I enjoy exploring symbols, long-term ethics, systems thinking, and myths. They are tools to navigate this rapidly shifting world and questions like the one above: How do we design and relate to digital systems? What kinds of tech and social patterns shape how we live and connect?

To me, it seems like the more we delve into machines, the more we reveal about the human condition. And about how little we actually know ourselves.

So welcome - let's explore together.

Warmly,
Elisa

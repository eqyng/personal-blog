33. On the rise of chatgptherapy

How much compute does emotional processing take?

#technology

#philosophy

#art

“Have you asked ChatGPT about this?“

is a phrase I find myself using when a friend is in an emotional quandary. I personally find it interesting to see what ChatGPT says, and sometimes it offers interesting perspectives I sometimes hadn’t considered.

But it did remind me - I recently came across this post by Mel Sellick:

The original Guardian article mentions how AI is being used for anything from a calm mediator for arguments, to a “teacher for emotional intelligence” to a grief companion. However, as Sellick points out, the question is whether we’re “starting to outsource our emotional labour”.

From the article’s standpoint, the positive uses seem compelling enough. Yvette, for instance, used ChatGPT as a way to confront her ex. Rather than agonising over how to craft an appropriately assertive response for hours, she vented, unrestrained, into the text field. Within seconds, it gave Yvette a more balanced message to send, without her needing to expend excess emotional energy. And there’s Tim, who leans on ChatGPT to help him debrief after marital conflicts, role-play difficult conversations or word loving messages to his wife. It’s a stark contrast to the hyper-masculine relationship guru advice he used to find.
